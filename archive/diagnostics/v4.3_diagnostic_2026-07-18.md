## 1. Headline stats reconciliation

**Finding: the dashboard is not broken.** Recomputing directly from raw `picks.json` and
also calling `compute_stats()` from the live `mlb_hr_model.py` module on the raw data
reproduces the dashboard's **39-141, −0.35u, −9.1%** exactly. The two figures only look
"mutually inconsistent" under a flat-1u-per-pick staking assumption — this project stakes
**quarter-Kelly** (capped at 0.03u/pick), so total capital at risk across 180 picks is far
below 180u.

| Check | Value |
|---|---|
| Bet-tier resolved, latest-window (`counts_toward_roi≠False`) | 180 picks |
| Record | 39-141 (21.7% win rate) |
| Total staked | **3.9140u** (avg 0.0217u/pick against a 0.03u cap — Kelly sizes rarely hit the cap) |
| Net P&L (Formula C, current code) | **−0.3549u** |
| ROI (net / staked) | **−9.07%** → rounds to dashboard's −9.1% |

Both `net` and `roi` were re-derived independently (manual recompute of `units_returned`
handling, and a direct call to `mlb_hr_model.compute_stats(picks)`) and both match the
dashboard to rounding. The apparent inconsistency in the task brief traces to comparing a
per-bet win/loss count against a Kelly-weighted dollar figure — not a bug.

**Formula check (the actual v4.2 bug, confirmed fixed):** re-running the *old* buggy
formula (`net = sum(units_returned)`, i.e. treating gross win return as profit) on the same
180 picks gives net **+0.43u**, ROI **+11.0%** — the opposite sign of the correct figure.
`check_results.py` (lines 336–339) and `mlb_hr_model.py` `compute_stats()` (lines 655–662)
both currently implement the corrected formula (win profit = `units_returned − units_staked`;
loss profit = `units_returned`, which is already `−stake`). No regression found — Formula C
is applied consistently in both code paths.

**Data-integrity side notes:**

  duplicates of a later-window pick for the same `(date, player_id, game_pk)` and are
  correctly excluded via `counts_toward_roi=False`. Dedup is working as designed.
- **Dashboard footer text bug (cosmetic, not the ROI bug):** `docs/index.html` and the
  report generator's `odds_note` string (mlb_hr_model.py:905) both say gated picks failed

| 20%+ | 1,298 | 26.7% | 18.6% | −8.1pp |

Same pattern as v4.1: underconfident at the low end, sharply overconfident at the high end.

**Logistic recalibration:** `logit(P(HR)) = a + b·logit(model_prob)`, fit by Newton–Raphson
on all 4,806 resolved picks (observed-information SEs):

| Param | Estimate | SE | vs. null |
|---|--:|--:|--:|
| Intercept `a` | −1.1481 | 0.1030 | z = −11.15 vs. 0 |
| Slope `b` | 0.4014 | 0.0511 | z = −11.73 vs. 1 (95% CI ≈ [0.30, 0.50]) |

Slope is well below 1 and the CI excludes 1 by a wide margin — **confirms overdispersion**:
the model's probability *spread* is roughly 2.5x too wide relative to what the outcomes
support, on top of the additive miscalibration.

| Metric | Value |
|---|--:|
| Brier — raw `model_prob` | 0.11108 |
| Brier — constant base-rate predictor (12.63%) | 0.11035 |
| Brier — in-sample recalibrated | 0.10864 |

**Notable:** the raw model's Brier score (0.11108) is *worse* than simply predicting the
league base rate on every pick (0.11035) — skill score ≈ **−0.007** (negative skill),
matching the v4.1 finding ("essentially no calibration advantage"). Recalibration flips this
to a small positive skill score (+0.016 in-sample; this is an in-sample fit, not a held-out
test, so treat the improvement as an upper bound).

**Recalibrated reference points:**

|---|--:|--:|--:|--:|--:|

| 1.0–1.5 | 1,936 | 16.5% | 14.8% | −1.7pp | 4.9% |

## 5. Gate diagnostics

| gate_failed value | n |
|---|--:|
| `max_odds` | 161 |
| `implied_prob_floor` | 30 |
| both | **structurally impossible** |

`gate_failed` is set by an `if`/`elif` in `run_model()` (mlb_hr_model.py:531–537):
`max_odds` is checked first; `implied_prob_floor` is only evaluated when odds ≤ 500 (i.e.
only reachable when `max_odds` did *not* fire). The field can hold at most one value per
pick by construction — "both" isn't a data question, it's a code-structure fact.

**Does the floor ever fire independently? Yes — 30 times**, and interestingly **all 30 have
`best_odds` exactly +500** (min=max=500). This is consistent with the documented ≥16%
devig floor (at +475 devigged implied ≈16.0%, passes; at +500 devigged implied ≈15.3%,
which is exactly the described partition point) — but in practice, live Caesars lines never
land in the +475–+499 gap at all; the floor's real-world trigger population is entirely
+500 lines. `max_odds` picks range +525 to +2000.

**Counterfactual — resolved gated-out picks staked at flat 1u:**

| Group | n | Record | Win% | ROI @ 1u |
|---|--:|--:|--:|--:|
| All gated | 189 | 11-178 | 5.8% | **−52.8%** |
| `max_odds` only | 159 | 6-153 | 3.8% | −62.7% |
| `implied_prob_floor` only | 30 | 5-25 | 16.7% | **+0.0%** (breakeven) |
| **Actual Bet-tier (passed gates)** | 180 | 39-141 | 21.7% | −3.8% @ flat 1u / −9.1% kelly-weighted |

The gates are doing real work: gated-out picks lose −52.8% at flat stakes vs. −3.8% for the
picks that passed, on an apples-to-apples flat-1u basis. Almost all of that gap comes from
`max_odds` (−62.7%, the bulk of gated volume); the `implied_prob_floor` sub-population is
actually close to breakeven (0.0% on n=30) — its exclusions are lower-conviction but not
clearly bad bets. If anything this suggests the +500 odds cap is carrying the gate's value
and the 16% floor is a weaker, arguably unnecessary filter on this sample size — though n=30
is too small to act on.

`sp_data_missing` is negligible across the board (probable pitchers are announced early

This suggests bullpen-stat gaps are a persistent ~11% data-source issue (missing team
pitching stats), not a same-day-timing issue that a later run resolves.

| Split | n | Pred | Actual | Diff |
|---|--:|--:|--:|--:|

Counterintuitively, missing-data picks are *better* calibrated than complete-data picks —

removes exactly the kind of multi-factor stacking shown in §4 to drive overprediction.

**Bet-tier check:** 0 of 273 raw Bet-tier picks have `sp_data_missing` or `bp_data_missing`
True — expected and confirmed, since `pitcher_data_missing` unconditionally downgrades `rec`
from `Bet` to `—` before the v4.3 gates even run (mlb_hr_model.py:502–504). No Bet-tier
ROI split by missing-data is possible; the design guarantees it's always "complete." As a
related data point: of the raw-edge≥5pp picks that got downgraded specifically for missing
pitcher data (not gate_failed), n=38, record 5-33 (13.2% win rate) — a small, weak-looking
sample, but directionally consistent with "missing pitcher data" being a legitimate reason
to not trust an edge.

## Summary of flags

1. **No ROI/net-units bug found.** The dashboard's −9.1% ROI and −0.35u net are mutually
   consistent under quarter-Kelly staking (total staked 3.914u across 180 picks, avg
   0.0217u/pick). The task brief's "flat staking" inconsistency premise doesn't hold for
   this project's actual staking method. The historical v4.2 gross-return-as-profit bug is
   confirmed fixed and consistently applied in both `check_results.py` and
   `mlb_hr_model.py::compute_stats()`.
2. **Cosmetic dashboard bug:** report footer text says implied-prob floor gate is "≥10%";
   actual constant (`MIN_DEVIGGED_IMPLIED`) is 16%. Text-only, doesn't affect any gating
   or staking logic. Not fixed here per task scope (analysis only).
3. **Model is overdispersed:** logistic recalibration slope 0.40 (SE 0.05), significantly
   below 1. Raw model has *negative* Brier skill vs. a constant base-rate predictor
   (0.11108 vs. 0.11035); recalibration flips this positive in-sample.
4. **Edge is non-monotonic** in both raw and recalibrated form; live Bet-tier ROI is best
   at 5–7.5pp (+11.5%) and worst at 10–15pp (−32.9%), matching v4.1/v4.2's documented
   pattern across three model versions now.
5. **Overprediction concentrates in factor-stacking**, cleanly monotonic with both total
   multiplier and count of simultaneous positive factors (0 positive: +4.9pp under; 7
   positive: −10.0pp over). Statcast-cap picks are 6.75x more overpredicted than uncapped.
   Coors picks ride along with general stacking rather than having an independent effect.
6. **`implied_prob_floor` gate looks weak** (breakeven at n=30, all exactly +500 odds)
   relative to `max_odds` (−62.7% for what it excludes) — worth more data before concluding,
   but the odds cap appears to be doing essentially all of the gates' protective work.
7. **`bp_data_missing` (~11%) is flat across windows**, not improving from early→mid as
   the v4.3 watch items expected — looks like a persistent data-source gap rather than a
   same-day timing issue. `sp_data_missing` is negligible (0.5%) and not a concern.
8. Missing-pitcher-data picks are, perhaps counterintuitively, *better calibrated* than
   complete-data picks — consistent with §5's finding that stacking (which missing-data
   defaults suppress) is the main driver of overprediction.
